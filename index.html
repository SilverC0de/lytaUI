Trading Strategies

Our day trading strategies and systems which are available and how they are used to make profits.

Scalping

Scalping is a day trading strategy that aims to achieve many small profits based on minimal price changes. Scalpers aim for a large quantity of trades, opening almost 'on a hunch', because there is no other way to navigate through the market noise. ‍

Reverse Trading

Reverse trading is also known as pull back trading, counter trend trading, and fading. In essence, this strategy attempts to profit from a reversal in trends in the markets.

Momentum Trading

Momentum trading is one of the more straightforward day trading strategies which searches for strong price moves paired with high volumes.

Manage risk

It is vital you sit down and develop a risk management strategy. This will ensure you only lose what you can afford. Without one of these, your time as a day trader could be extremely short-lived.

Don’t concentrate on the money

Having money at the forefront of your mind could make you do reckless things, like taking tiny profits in fear of losing what you’ve already won, or jumping straight in so you don’t miss a move.

Know when to stop

If the strategy isn’t working, we don’t keep throwing money at it. We go back to the drawing board and think again.
